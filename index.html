CBS Nurture Sequence

What happens after someone gives us an email

Three automated email tracks that keep CBS in front of an interested buyer and turn quiet contacts into consult requests. This is the sequence, start to handoff.

JUNE 15, 2026  ·  PREPARED BY JOSH AGEE, VINYL MARKETING
· The idea

Lead with value. Earn the conversation.

A contact gives us an email, and today nothing happens. The nurture sequence fixes that: every email delivers something useful first, builds credibility over a few sends, then makes a low-pressure ask. No hard selling, no "you are subscribed" receipts.

A contact leaves a track and routes to sales the moment they raise a real hand, by requesting a consult or submitting the Contact Us form. They should never get a generic nurture email after that.

Priority and fastest win: the Track A welcome email. A signup currently lands on the homepage with no response. The welcome email is the first thing to build.

01 The Welcome Email

The first send, built right.

CBS runs one catch-all newsletter, so the welcome email speaks to everyone without diluting value. A scannable, sectioned layout lets each reader self-select their lane. The more specific the label, the higher the value: "operational excellence" is a weak signal, "aerospace and defense compliance" is a strong one.

01

Warm, short open

One or two lines. Confirm they are in, then pivot straight to value.

02

Three labeled value blocks

One per vertical: Private Equity Aerospace & Defense Lean & Op-Ex. Each links two or three of the strongest CBS resources for that reader.

03

One primary next step

A soft consult offer: 15 minutes with an operator to talk through their situation, framed as help, not a demo.

04

Footer

Who CBS is in one line, plus an honest unsubscribe.
